Mechanism Aluminum Iron Linkage Engineering

Concept Overview

This project explores the aesthetics and function of classic mechanical articulation through handcrafted metalwork, celebrating simplicity, strength, and kinetic elegance.

Materiality and Construction

  • Aluminum Rails: Lightweight, rigid frame for smooth motion.
  • Iron Components: Manually shaped for complex linkage movement.
  • Riveted Axes: Metal rivets as both connectors and pivot points.

Kinetic Behavior

  • Forms a linkage mechanism capable of controlled extension and contraction.
  • Relies on pure mechanical interaction—no motors or electronics.

Process

Design Intent

A study in mechanical minimalism: how basic forms and manual techniques create expressive, functional mechanisms inspired by historical engineering.

Conceptual Themes

  • Precision & Imperfection: Manual work gives each joint unique character.
  • Force & Permanence: Rivets create inseparable, lasting joints.
  • Motion as Expression: The act of movement reveals the beauty of mechanics.

Technical Features

Linkage System

Complex mechanical linkage that enables controlled extension and contraction movements with precise geometric relationships.

Rivet Joints

Carefully positioned rivets serve as reliable pivot points, allowing smooth rotational movement between components.

Precision Angles

Meticulously calculated and manufactured bending angles ensure optimal mechanical advantage and movement efficiency.
