MEDIEVAL GAUNTLET

[ HANDCRAFTED WEARABLE ARMOR / METALWORK / MECHANICAL DESIGN ]

2024

I handcrafted this wearable medieval gauntlet using iron, aluminum, screws, and a leather glove.

Each joint is carefully designed for flexibility, while the interlocking metal plates provide solid protection.

The combination of aluminum for lightness and iron for strength ensures both functionality and an authentic medieval aesthetic.

This piece is not just a recreation of armor but an exploration of historical craftsmanship and mechanical design.

ROLE

Designer, Metalworker, Craftsman

DURATION

6 weeks

MATERIALS

Iron, Aluminum, Screws, Leather Glove

FOCUS

Historical Craftsmanship, Mechanical Design

Overview

This medieval gauntlet project is a study in both historical armor and modern handcraft. The design process involved researching authentic medieval gauntlets, selecting appropriate materials, and engineering each joint for both flexibility and protection.

Design Features

  • Hand-forged iron and aluminum plates
  • Flexible joint construction
  • Interlocking plate system for protection
  • Leather glove base for comfort
  • Authentic medieval aesthetic

Craftsmanship & Process

Each plate was individually cut, shaped, and riveted. The assembly required careful alignment to ensure both movement and durability. The final gauntlet is fully wearable and demonstrates the intersection of historical research and hands-on making.
