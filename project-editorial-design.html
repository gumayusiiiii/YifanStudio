Shared Heater

[ PRODUCT DESIGN / PROTOTYPING / SOCIAL INNOVATION ]

From the earliest campfires, warmth and human connection have been inseparable. Guided by this insight, I re-engineered the conventional, one-directional space heater into Shared Heater, a 360-degree electric heat source that invites people to gather and converse without the hazards of open flame.

ROLE

Product Designer, Engineer, Prototyper

DURATION

8 weeks

TOOLS

SolidWorks, 3D Printing, Embedded Programming, CFD Simulation

YEAR

2025

Project Narrative

Shared Heater transforms the conventional heater into a 360° social centerpiece—safe, electric, and inviting. Inspired by the timeless link between warmth and human connection.
Design Insight
From the earliest campfires, warmth and human connection have been inseparable. Shared Heater invites people to gather and converse without the hazards of open flame.
Form Exploration
The radial fin layout projects heat in every direction, turning the device into a social focal point—much like a modern campfire.
Final Product
The final design offers a cleaner, safer, and more portable alternative to traditional fire pits—rekindling the timeless ritual of gathering around shared warmth.
User Experience
User testing in outdoor cafés and studio courtyards confirmed the concept: the heater draws people into a comfortable radius, encouraging spontaneous conversation even on cold evenings.

Design Philosophy

The design approach emphasized letting the artwork speak while providing a sophisticated framework for content organization. We developed a modular grid system that could adapt to different types of content, from full-bleed artwork to detailed text passages.

Special attention was paid to paper selection and printing techniques to ensure the highest quality reproduction of the featured artworks.
