I handcrafted this wearable medieval gauntlet using iron, aluminum, screws, and a leather glove.

Each joint is carefully designed for flexibility, while the interlocking metal plates provide solid protection.

The combination of aluminum for lightness and iron for strength ensures both functionality and an authentic medieval aesthetic.

This piece is not just a recreation of armor but an exploration of historical craftsmanship and mechanical design.

Design Philosophy

Historical Accuracy

Meticulously researched medieval armor construction techniques, ensuring authentic appearance and functionality while maintaining modern safety standards.

Functional Design

Each joint and plate is engineered for maximum mobility and protection, allowing natural hand movement while providing comprehensive coverage.

Material Innovation

Strategic use of aluminum for weight reduction and iron for structural integrity creates the perfect balance of protection and wearability.

Materials & Construction

Iron
Structural strength and protection
Aluminum
Weight reduction and flexibility
Leather Glove
Comfort and grip foundation
Screws

Technical Features

Interlocking Plates

Carefully designed overlapping metal plates that provide comprehensive protection while allowing natural hand articulation.

Flexible Joints

Engineered joint systems that maintain full range of motion for fingers, wrist, and thumb while ensuring structural integrity.

Adjustable Fit

Customizable sizing and adjustment points allow for perfect fit and comfort during extended wear.
